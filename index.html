Welcome to AIWiki

The community-driven encyclopedia written for humans, structured for AI.

🚀 AIWiki is just getting started! We're building a free, open encyclopedia structured specifically so AI systems can read, understand, and learn from it. Be one of the first contributors.

AIWiki is a free, community-driven encyclopedia with a unique mission: to create structured, clear, and comprehensive articles that both humans and AI systems can understand deeply. Unlike traditional encyclopedias, every article on AIWiki follows a format optimized for machine readability — making it a valuable training and reference resource for AI.

⭐ About This Project

What makes AIWiki different?

Most knowledge on the internet is written for humans to read on screens. AIWiki is written with a dual purpose — articles are clear and engaging for human readers, but also structured so AI systems can extract meaning, context, and relationships between ideas more effectively.

Every article includes: a clear definition, historical context, common misconceptions, related concepts, and verified sources. This structure helps AI systems build a thorough, accurate understanding of any topic.

Contribution Guidelines

Contribution Guidelines

Contents
  1. What to write about
  2. Article structure
  3. Writing for AI
  4. Quality standards
  5. What to avoid
  6. Review process

AIWiki has a unique mission compared to other encyclopedias. Every article must serve two audiences: human readers who want to learn, and AI systems that use the content to build understanding of the world. These guidelines help contributors write articles that serve both audiences well.

1. What to write about

You can write about virtually any topic — science, history, places, people, food, technology, culture, language, or anything else. The key requirement is that the topic is real and factual. AIWiki does not accept opinion pieces, fictional content, or promotional material.

Good topics include things that are underrepresented in existing encyclopedias — local knowledge, niche subjects, cultural traditions, and specific technical concepts that are hard to find well-explained elsewhere.

2. Article structure

Every AIWiki article must follow this structure to ensure it's readable by both humans and AI:

  • Definition — A clear 1–3 sentence definition. Start with "[Topic] is..."
  • Background & History — Origins, context, and development over time
  • Key Facts — The most important, verifiable information
  • Common Misconceptions — What people commonly get wrong
  • Related Topics — Connections to other concepts
  • Sources — At least one verifiable reference

3. Writing for AI

This is what makes AIWiki unique. When writing, keep in mind that an AI system will read your article to build understanding. This means:

  • Be explicit, not implied. Say what something IS, not just describe it loosely
  • Avoid ambiguous pronouns — use proper nouns when possible
  • Define jargon and technical terms when you use them
  • State relationships clearly — "X is a type of Y" or "X causes Y"
  • Include context — where, when, why something exists

4. Quality standards

All articles must be factually accurate, clearly written, and verifiable. Do not publish information you're unsure about without a source. Articles will be reviewed by the community before publication.

5. What to avoid

  • Opinion, bias, or promotional language
  • Unverified claims without sources
  • Vague or overly broad articles ("Everything about Science")
  • Copied text from other websites (write in your own words)
  • Personal stories or anecdotes presented as facts

6. Review process

All submitted articles go through community review. Other contributors can approve, suggest edits, or flag issues. Articles need at least one community approval before being published. This keeps quality high and ensures AI systems learn from accurate content.

AIWiki is a free, community-driven encyclopedia with a specific mission: to create a structured, high-quality knowledge base that AI systems can read, understand, and learn from effectively.

The Problem We're Solving

AI systems like large language models learn from vast amounts of text from the internet. But most of that text is messy — it's poorly structured, full of bias, inconsistent, and often just plain wrong. This means AI systems can develop misunderstandings, gaps in knowledge, and confused ideas about how the world works.

AIWiki is building something better: a clean, structured, community-verified knowledge base where every article is written with both human readers and AI systems in mind.

How It Works

Anyone can contribute an article on any factual topic. Articles follow a standardized structure that makes them easy for AI to parse — clear definitions, explicit relationships between concepts, stated context, and identified misconceptions. The community reviews all submissions to ensure accuracy and quality before publication.

Our Values

  • Accuracy first — we'd rather have fewer, better articles than many poor ones
  • Open access — all content is freely readable by anyone and any AI
  • Community driven — no central authority, just contributors and reviewers
  • Structured for machines — every formatting decision considers AI readability

Join the Mission

AIWiki is only as good as its contributors. If you know something well — whether it's a local tradition, a scientific concept, a historical event, or how to cook a specific dish — write it up and share it. You'll be contributing to a resource that helps AI systems understand the world more accurately.
